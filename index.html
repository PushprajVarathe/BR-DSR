About The Project


The project involves the Rehabilitation and Upgradation of Lateri Shamshabad Road under Package-25A, facilitated through a public-private partnership by Madhya Pradesh Road Development Corporation Ltd (MPRDC) and funded by the Asian Development Bank. Private sector bids were solicited on November 24, 2021, resulting in the award of the project to BR Goyal Infrastructure Ltd. on May 13, 2022.


For project execution, a special purpose vehicle named BR-DSR Lateri Shamshabad Pvt. Ltd. has been established and entrusted with concessionaire responsibilities. The appointed date for project commencement was achieved on November 19, 2022. The project is being overseen by consultant M/s Bloom Companies, LLC, and is following a Hybrid Annuity financial model. The project's road begins at a Cross-junction near Lateri Town, connecting Sironj - Maksudangarh Road (NH-752B) to Daulatpura, Jatpura, Baroda, and ending at a T-junction in Mohanpura-Shamshabad Road. The existing length and design length of the road are both 35.600 Km. It consists of a 4-lane section (0.300 Km), a 2-lane section (0.700 Km), and an Intermediate lane (34.600 Km), with the existing pavement rated in fair to poor condition.


Key roles in the project include Madhya Pradesh Road Development Corporation Ltd (MPRDC), Asian Development Bank, BR Goyal Infrastructure Ltd., BR-DSR Lateri Shamshabad Pvt. Ltd. as the concessionaire, and consultant M/s Bloom Companies, LLC. The project follows a Hybrid Annuity model for financing and aims to enhance road infrastructure for better connectivity and travel.

Bloom Companies

Bloom Companies, LLC was formed by Mathew Tharaniyil in May of 1998. Prior to founding Bloom, Mathew worked for the City of Milwaukee and played an integral role in the City's infrastructure design. When Bloom began operations, the primary focus of the company was to provide quality services for bridge and structural engineering. With the addition of qualified professionals, Bloom has been able to expand its service offerings. Today the company offers a wide range of engineering, architecture and construction management services to a wide variety of clients. In 2008, Bloom ventured into the international market when presented with an opportunity to work on a long span bridge in Vladivostok, Russia. Now Bloom has an established presence in India with multiple branch offices supporting multiple infrastructure improvement projects
